Emily Boeke

I am currently working as a data science intern at Dotdash. I recently completed my PhD in a cognitive neuroscience lab at NYU. I studied the brain circuitry underlying anxiety and fear learning (learning that certain stimuli are threatening or safe). As an undergraduate, I studied biopsychology at Tufts University.

Other Projects

Predicting the outcomes of asylum cases

Collaborative project with Aspen Yoo and Ruby Fu, advised by Professors Daniel Chen and Elliott Ash. In this project, we predicted the outcomes of court cases of refugees seeking asylum using immigration court data from 1985-2013.

Brooklyn rent stabilization map

I made an interactive map showing the distribution of rent stabilized buildings across Brooklyn zip codes. I used the 2016 rent stabilized building list put out by NYC Housing Preservation and Development (HPD) to compute the number of stabilized buildings per zip code, and obtained information on total number of buildings per zip code via the NYC OpenData website.
